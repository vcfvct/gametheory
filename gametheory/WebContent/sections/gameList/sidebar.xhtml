  • Rules

    In this game, two companies, with decisions by their CEOs, obtained permission to manufacture in a developing country on the premise of at least one must provide schooling or pay a fine if neither provides schooling.
    Payoffs, profits or losses, will be reported after each round and are cumulative. Payoffs are a function of a static set of earnings minus the cost of schooling and a fine if no schooling is funded by one of the CEOs.
    The static earnings are sufficient so that the cost of schooling and fines will only diminish profits. The cost for schooling is slightly higher than the fines.
    A company will be rewarded if it provides schooling in any two of four rounds. The reward is an incentive. It means no more fines in the remained of the four rounds. Also, if the other CEO provides the schooling and one CEO has two periods of schooling cost, no fines.
    Thus, the higher the profits in any four rounds mean both the company and the country benefit, although the benefit may come from the other CEO spending on schooling.
